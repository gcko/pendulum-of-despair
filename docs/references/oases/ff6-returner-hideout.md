The **Returner Hideout** , also known as **Returners' Hideout** , is a location in _[Final Fantasy VI](/wiki/Final_Fantasy_VI "Final Fantasy VI")_ where the [Returners](/wiki/Returners "Returners"), a resistance group formed to counter the [Empire](/wiki/Gestahlian_Empire "Gestahlian Empire"), has its headquarters. It is located in the [Sabre Mountains](/wiki/Sabre_Mountains "Sabre Mountains"), and contains a [shop](/wiki/Shop "Shop") and an [inn](/wiki/Inn "Inn"). The [Lethe River](/wiki/Lethe_River "Lethe River") flows past the hideout, and the Returners keep rafts so they can escape in an emergency.

## Story[[](https://finalfantasy.fandom.com/wiki/Returner_Hideout?veaction=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Story")]
**[Spoiler warning](/wiki/Final_Fantasy_Wiki:Spoiler_Policy "Final Fantasy Wiki:Spoiler Policy"): _Plot and/or ending details follow._** _(Skip section)_
[](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/finalfantasy/images/e/e2/Banon_Meets_Terra_from_FFVI_Pixel_Remaster.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20220307211305) [](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/finalfantasy/images/e/e2/Banon_Meets_Terra_from_FFVI_Pixel_Remaster.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20220307211305) [](/wiki/File:Banon_Meets_Terra_from_FFVI_Pixel_Remaster.jpg)
Banon meets Terra.
[Edgar](/wiki/Edgar_Roni_Figaro "Edgar Roni Figaro"), [Locke](/wiki/Locke_Cole "Locke Cole"), and [Sabin](/wiki/Sabin_Rene_Figaro "Sabin Rene Figaro") bring a girl named [Terra](/wiki/Terra_Branford "Terra Branford") to the hideout so they could introduce her to [Banon](/wiki/Banon "Banon"), head of the resistance group, due to her unheard-of ability to use [magic](/wiki/Magic_\(term\) "Magic \(term\)") and seeming ability to communicate with [espers](/wiki/Esper_\(Final_Fantasy_VI\) "Esper \(Final Fantasy VI\)"). 
Banon believes Terra is the Returners' hope to push back the Empire and stop them from conquering the world, but Terra is uncertain if she wants to get involved in the war. While the group is at the hideout, the Empire conquers [South Figaro](/wiki/South_Figaro "South Figaro") and begins a march on the Returners. With their hideout in jeopardy, Terra flees down the [Lethe River](/wiki/Lethe_River "Lethe River") with Edgar, Sabin, and Banon, while Locke returns to South Figaro to sabotage the Empire's advance. 
In the [World of Ruin](/wiki/World_of_Ruin_\(Final_Fantasy_VI\) "World of Ruin \(Final Fantasy VI\)"), the approximate location of the Sabre Mountains is covered by ocean, and the Returner Hideout has presumably sank beneath the waters during the [cataclysm](/wiki/Cataclysm_\(Final_Fantasy_VI\) "Cataclysm \(Final Fantasy VI\)").

### Items[[](https://finalfantasy.fandom.com/wiki/Returner_Hideout?veaction=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Items")]
[](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/finalfantasy/images/9/95/Returner_Storage_Room_from_FFVI_Pixel_Remaster.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20220307212021) [](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/finalfantasy/images/9/95/Returner_Storage_Room_from_FFVI_Pixel_Remaster.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20220307212021) [](/wiki/File:Returner_Storage_Room_from_FFVI_Pixel_Remaster.jpg)
The Returner Hideout.
A pot in the main room contains a [Green Cherry](/wiki/Green_Cherry_\(Final_Fantasy_VI\) "Green Cherry \(Final Fantasy VI\)"). Banon's room has a chest with a [Hi-Potion](/wiki/Hi-Potion_\(Final_Fantasy_VI\) "Hi-Potion \(Final Fantasy VI\)"), and the room Terra rests in has a [Phoenix Down](/wiki/Phoenix_Down_\(Final_Fantasy_VI\) "Phoenix Down \(Final Fantasy VI\)"). A rear storage room has three chests with a Phoenix Down, [Knight's Code](/wiki/Knight%27s_Code_\(Final_Fantasy_VI\) "Knight's Code \(Final Fantasy VI\)"), and [Air Knife](/wiki/Air_Knife_\(Final_Fantasy_VI\) "Air Knife \(Final Fantasy VI\)"), and the containers in the room contain an [Ether](/wiki/Ether_\(Final_Fantasy_VI\) "Ether \(Final Fantasy VI\)") and an [Antidote](/wiki/Antidote_\(Final_Fantasy_VI\) "Antidote \(Final Fantasy VI\)"). 
There is a hidden passage in the storage room leading to one more treasure. On the right side of the pile of crates, the player can walk into the wall down and to the right, eventually finding a chest with a [White Cape](/wiki/White_Cape_\(Final_Fantasy_VI\) "White Cape \(Final Fantasy VI\)"), a rare and valuable Relic at this early point of the game. 
Depending on the player's choices in helping Banon or not, they can receive a [Genji Glove](/wiki/Genji_Glove_\(Final_Fantasy_VI\) "Genji Glove \(Final Fantasy VI\)") or a [Gauntlet](/wiki/Gauntlet_\(Final_Fantasy_VI\) "Gauntlet \(Final Fantasy VI\)"). The Gauntlet is received by agreeing to help Banon, while the Genji Glove is received by saying no to him three times, or by saying no once and then speaking to a Returner in the storage room. The player can only acquire one of these Relics; if they acquire the Genji Glove from the Returner and then agree to help, Banon will not give them the Gauntlet. 
Which item to take is a matter of personal preference, though a point of note on rarity - the Gauntlet is a very rare relic only obtainable at two other points much later in the game, while the Genji Glove is farmable. Additionally, the player can find a Genji Glove in the [Cave to the Sealed Gate](/wiki/Cave_to_the_Sealed_Gate "Cave to the Sealed Gate"), much sooner than they will have a chance to get a Gauntlet if they pass up the one Banon gives them. 
### Quests[[](https://finalfantasy.fandom.com/wiki/Returner_Hideout?veaction=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Quests")]
#### Scrap of Paper[[](https://finalfantasy.fandom.com/wiki/Returner_Hideout?veaction=edit&section=7 "Edit section: Scrap of Paper")]
[](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/finalfantasy/images/4/49/Banon_Paper_from_FFVI_Pixel_Remaster.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20220307211306) [](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/finalfantasy/images/4/49/Banon_Paper_from_FFVI_Pixel_Remaster.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20220307211306) [](/wiki/File:Banon_Paper_from_FFVI_Pixel_Remaster.jpg)
Banon notices the piece of paper during the meeting.
When Terra is alone in the party, a [Scrap of Paper](/wiki/Final_Fantasy_VI_items#Scrap_of_Paper "Final Fantasy VI items") can be found hidden next to the table in the Returner Hideout prior to the meeting that takes place with Banon. The player has the choice of either leaving the piece of paper on the floor or tossing it in the trash. Choosing to leave the paper on the floor will make Banon say a few more lines before the start of the meeting which will make Terra chuckle. The Scrap of Paper is a key item and because of it being associated with an event, regardless of the player's choice, it is not added to the key item inventory. 
Finding the scrap of paper or not has no impact on gameplay or story beyond the brief addition of Banon noticing it. 
#### Terra's choice[[](https://finalfantasy.fandom.com/wiki/Returner_Hideout?veaction=edit&section=8 "Edit section: Terra's choice")]
[](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/finalfantasy/images/d/df/Banon_and_Terra_from_FFVI_Pixel_Remaster.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20220307211304) [](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/finalfantasy/images/d/df/Banon_and_Terra_from_FFVI_Pixel_Remaster.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20220307211304) [](/wiki/File:Banon_and_Terra_from_FFVI_Pixel_Remaster.jpg)
Banon attempting to persuade Terra to join the Returner's cause.
When the player reaches the Returner hideout, they ask Terra to consider joining them to help combat the [Gestahlian Empire](/wiki/Gestahlian_Empire "Gestahlian Empire") and perhaps learn more about her powers. After speaking to Locke, Edgar and Sabin throughout the hideout, the guard at the door lets her step outside to speak to Banon, who asks if she will aid them. 
If the player agrees, Banon will give Terra a [Gauntlet](/wiki/Gauntlet_\(Final_Fantasy_VI\) "Gauntlet \(Final Fantasy VI\)"). If the player refuses three times, or if they refuse once and then speak to the Returner in the storage room, they receive a [Genji Glove](/wiki/Genji_Glove_\(Final_Fantasy_VI\) "Genji Glove \(Final Fantasy VI\)"). 
If the player agrees to aid Banon, a prolonged scene will occur of the Returners meet to discuss what to do, which is interrupted when a wounded Returner arrives and informs them that South Figaro has been invaded by the Empire. If the player answers no to Banon three times, this scene is skilled to the arrival of the wounded Returner. Afterward the decision is made to flee to along the [Lethe River](/wiki/Lethe_River "Lethe River"). The player is then put in charge of a party of Terra, Edgar, Sabin, and Banon, in the back hall of the hideout. 
When the player boards the raft on the river, it is an instant Game Over if Banon is ever KO'd. The player may wish to go outside to the world map where enemies are not as powerful and level grind a bit for safety. Be aware however that even though the game has not informed the player not to let Banon be KO'd, the condition is applicable as long as he is in the party, so if he is defeated outside it will still be a Game Over. 
#### Gau and Mog[[](https://finalfantasy.fandom.com/wiki/Returner_Hideout?veaction=edit&section=9 "Edit section: Gau and Mog")]
After the [Battle for the Frozen Esper](/wiki/Battle_for_the_Frozen_Esper "Battle for the Frozen Esper"), the imperial blocking access to Mt. Kolts leave, and the player can take a long walk back to the hideout through the [South Figaro Cave](/wiki/South_Figaro_Cave "South Figaro Cave") and Mt. Kolts, and board the raft on the Lethe River again. The raft disembarks them at [Gau's father's house](/wiki/Gau%27s_father%27s_house "Gau's father's house"). At this point, traveling far south past [Doma Castle](/wiki/Doma_Castle "Doma Castle") and the [Phantom Forest](/wiki/Phantom_Forest "Phantom Forest") to [Baren Falls](/wiki/Baren_Falls "Baren Falls"), where they can leap off to the [Veldt](/wiki/Veldt "Veldt"). To get back to Figaro, they can leap into the [Serpent Trench](/wiki/Serpent_Trench "Serpent Trench") to [Nikeah](/wiki/Nikeah "Nikeah") and board a ship to South Figaro. This is a long journey, but it allows the player access to the Veldt to continue building [Gau](/wiki/Gau "Gau")'s skills before they acquire the airship. 
Once [Mog](/wiki/Mog_\(Final_Fantasy_VI_party_member\) "Mog \(Final Fantasy VI party member\)") has joined the party, he can learn the Water Harmony [Dance](/wiki/Dance_\(Final_Fantasy_VI\) "Dance \(Final Fantasy VI\)") by going down the Lethe River or fighting in the Serpent Trench. Therse are the only two ways the Dance can be learned in the Super Nintendo and Playstation versions; in the _Advance_ version and mobile and [Steam](/wiki/Steam_\(service\) "Steam \(service\)") releases, the battle with [Leviathan](/wiki/Leviathan_\(Final_Fantasy_VI_boss\) "Leviathan \(Final Fantasy VI boss\)") is the third way. However, there is a quicker way to learn the Water Rondo now that the player has the airship, so going down the Lethe River is more time-consuming.

## Etymology[[](https://finalfantasy.fandom.com/wiki/Returner_Hideout?veaction=edit&section=13 "Edit section: Etymology")]
[[view](https://finalfantasy.fandom.com/wiki/Etymology:Returner) · [edit](https://finalfantasy.fandom.com/wiki/Etymology:Returner?action=edit) · [purge](https://finalfantasy.fandom.com/wiki/Etymology:Returner?action=purge)]_Returner_ means "to go" or "come back", as to an earlier condition or place. 
The Returners are trying to return things to the way they were before the empire's conquests, and return it to peaceful times. In the World of Ruin, the Returners' "spirit" lingers, with Celes saying she wants to return the world to the state before Kefka ruined it. Kefka brings death and destruction, while the party wants to return life into the dying world, finally materializing it in the ending. 
The word "returner" is used throughout the game not simply referring to the organization, but many NPCs in the World of Ruin mention their wish to return to the "good old days" (the World of Balance).
