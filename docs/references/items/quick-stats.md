## Final Fantasy VI (45 items)

**Sources:** `ff6-items.md` (Fandom), `ff6-items-gamefaqs.md` (GameFAQs Pixel Remaster)

| Type | Count | Examples |
|------|-------|---------|
| Healing (HP) | 9 | Potion, Hi-Potion, X-Potion, Dried Meat, Sleeping Bag, Tent |
| MP Restoration | 3 | Ether, Hi-Ether, X-Ether |
| Revival | 1 | Phoenix Down |
| Status Cure | 6 | Antidote, Eye Drops, Echo Screen, Gold Needle, Green Cherry, Remedy |
| Battle / Thrown | 4 | Super Ball, Smoke Bomb, Teleport Stone, Magicite Shard |
| Key Items | 17 | Opera Record, Pendant, Rust-Rid, Lump of Metal, Coral, Old Clock Key, etc. |
| Miscellaneous | 5 | Rename Card, Edgar's Coin, Slave Crown, etc. |

**Design Notes:**
- Healing is tiered (Potion → Hi-Potion → X-Potion) with a percentage-based top tier
- Remedy cures all statuses (single universal cure at premium cost)
- Status cures are 1:1 (Antidote → Poison, Eye Drops → Blind, etc.)
- Key items are heavily narrative — many are fetch quest objects
- No permanent stat boost items (stats grow via Esper bonuses at level-up)

## Final Fantasy IV (74 items)

**Source:** `ff4-items.md` (Fandom)

| Type | Count | Examples |
|------|-------|---------|
| Healing (HP) | 12 | Potion, Hi-Potion, X-Potion, Elixir, Megalixir, Cottage |
| MP Restoration | 4 | Ether, Hi-Ether, Turbo Ether, Elixir (dual) |
| Revival | 1 | Phoenix Down |
| Status Cure | 7 | Antidote, Cross, Alarm Clock, Unicorn Horn, Maiden's Kiss, Diet Ration, Remedy |
| Battle / Thrown | 18 | Bomb Fragment, Antarctic Wind, Arctic Wind, Zeus's Wrath, Blue/Red/White Fang, Bomb Crank, Light Curtain, Lunar Curtain, Spider Silk, Bacchus's Wine, Hermes Sandals, Bronze Hourglass, Silver Hourglass, Gold Hourglass |
| Key Items | 11 | Magma Stone, Earth Crystal, Dark Matter, Luca's Necklace, etc. |
| Miscellaneous | 21 | Summoning items (Bomb, Cockatrice, Coeurl Whisker), Decoy, Emergency Exit, Gysahl Greens, Siren, Tent, etc. |

**Design Notes:**
- Richest battle item system of the four — 18 throwable/usable combat items
- Elemental battle items (Fang series, Wind series) give non-casters offense options
- Hourglass series (Bronze/Silver/Gold) inflicts time-based statuses
- Status cures more varied than FF6 (Cross for curse, Alarm Clock for sleep)
- Summoning items are a unique category (consumable monster summons)

## Chrono Trigger (32 items)

**Source:** `chrono-trigger-items.md` (GameFAQs DS guide)

| Type | Count | Examples |
|------|-------|---------|
| Healing (HP) | 4 | Potion, Mid-Potion, Hi-Potion, Lapis (party heal) |
| MP Restoration | 3 | Ether, Mid-Ether, Hi-Ether |
| Revival | 1 | Athenian Water |
| Status Cure | 1 | Panacea (cures all) |
| Battle / Utility | 4 | Barrier Sphere, Shield Sphere, Shelter, Ambrosia |
| Stat Boost (permanent) | 6 | Strength Capsule, Magic Capsule, Speed Capsule, Power Tab, Magic Tab, Speed Tab |
| Miscellaneous | 13 | Elixir, Megalixir, and various special/rare items |

**Design Notes:**
- Most streamlined item system — only 32 consumables total
- Single universal status cure (Panacea) instead of per-status items
- ONLY game with permanent stat boost capsules (Tabs/Capsules)
- Lapis (party-wide heal) is unique — no equivalent in the other games
- Barrier/Shield Spheres provide temporary buffs (defense strategy items)
- Fewer items = less inventory management, more focus on Tech combos

## Secret of Mana (23 consumables + 72 weapon orbs)

**Source:** `secret-of-mana-items.md` (Fandom)

| Type | Count | Examples |
|------|-------|---------|
| Healing (HP) | 4 | Candy, Chocolate, Royal Jam, Cup of Wishes |
| MP Restoration | 1 | Faerie Walnut |
| Revival | 0 | (no dedicated revival item — Cup of Wishes revives + heals) |
| Status Cure | 1 | Medical Herb |
| Battle / Support | 2 | Barrel, Mystic Book |
| Key Items — Tools | 4 | Magic Rope, Flammie Drum, Moogle Belt, Minor Mallet |
| Key Items — Mana Power | 8 | Water, Earth, Wind, Fire, Light, Darkness, Moon, Wood |
| Key Items — Story | 4 | Water Seed, Fire Seed, Sea Hare's Tail, Gold Tower Key |
| Weapon Orbs | 72 | 8 weapon types × 9 upgrade orbs each |

**Design Notes:**
- Smallest consumable set (23) — action RPG design needs fewer items
- Food-themed healing (Candy, Chocolate, Royal Jam) — charming flavor
- Weapon Orb system (72 orbs) is the primary progression mechanic
- Mana Power items permanently boost spell schools (8 elements)
- No permanent stat boost consumables — progression is equipment-driven
- Cup of Wishes serves double duty (heal + revive)

## Design Takeaways for Pendulum of Despair

| Pattern | Games Using It | Implication |
|---------|---------------|-------------|
| Tiered potions (3 tiers) | FF4, FF6, CT | Standard — players expect Potion → Hi-Potion → X-Potion |
| Universal status cure | FF6 (Remedy), CT (Panacea) | Expensive all-in-one alongside cheap per-status cures |
| Elemental battle items | FF4 (18 items!) | Gives non-casters magical offense; creates economy decisions |
| Permanent stat capsules | CT only | Powerful reward loop; encourages exploration |
| Weapon upgrade materials | SoM (72 orbs) | Crafting-adjacent system; good for Lira's Forgewright |
| Key item count | 11–17 per game | Keep manageable; mostly narrative triggers |
| Total consumable count | 23–74 range | FF6's 45 is the sweet spot for a turn-based JRPG |
