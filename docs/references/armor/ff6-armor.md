This is a list of shields, headgear, and body armor in _[Final Fantasy VI](/wiki/Final_Fantasy_VI "Final Fantasy VI")_. In the SNES version, Evasion bonuses have no effect due to the [Evade bug](/wiki/Evade_bug "Evade bug"). This has been fixed in the _[Advance](/wiki/Finest_Fantasy_for_Advance "Finest Fantasy for Advance")_ version onward. Many [shops](/wiki/Shop "Shop") change their inventory, and thus many of the equipment pieces listed on this page as being sold in a location will not be available for purchase at all times.

## Shields[[](https://finalfantasy.fandom.com/wiki/Final_Fantasy_VI_armor?veaction=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Shields")]
[](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/finalfantasy/images/d/db/Shield_from_FFVI_icon.png/revision/latest?cb=20120610072147)[](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/finalfantasy/images/a/a0/FFVI_Shields_Sprites.png/revision/latest?cb=20130303212330 "Different colored shields.")Different colored shields.
Shields do not have a set appearance; the shield sprite that appears when an attack is blocked with a shield depends on what is being blocked. The first shield in the picture to the right is the one used to block physical attacks and enemies' special attacks. If a magic spell and/or an enemy spell is blocked by a shield, it takes on the palette of the spell being blocked. This no longer occurred in the 2014 version. 
The "shield bug" involves the Force Shield and the Cursed Shield. Normally, when equipped, these shields either grant a positive status effect ([shell](/wiki/Shell_\(Final_Fantasy_VI_status\) "Shell \(Final Fantasy VI status\)") in the Force Shield's case) or inflict a variety of negative status effects (in the Cursed Shield's case). However, when equipped in the middle of [battle](/wiki/Final_Fantasy_VI_battle_system "Final Fantasy VI battle system"), instead of applying the statuses the shields are supposed to, they instead provide _immunity_ to them.

## Other appearances[[](https://finalfantasy.fandom.com/wiki/Final_Fantasy_VI_armor?veaction=edit&section=14 "Edit section: Other appearances")]
Many of the armor in _Final Fantasy VI_ reappeared in spinoff titles as weapons used by characters in those appearances. Armor introduced in _Final Fantasy VI_ , such as the Minerva Bustier, went on to become common recurring armor in other titles.
