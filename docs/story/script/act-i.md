> shift, and an artifact found in a dead mine sets everything in motion.

## Scene 1: The Ember Vein

<!-- Scene: ember_vein_tutorial | Tier: 3 | Trigger: game_start -->
<!-- Location: Ember Vein (beneath Ironmouth mine) | Party: Edren, Cael -->
<!-- Variants: none (fixed opening) -->
<!-- Cross-ref: outline.md § Act I The Opening, dungeons-world.md § 1 Ember Vein -->

*(Darkness. A lantern gutters. Two figures descend a rough-hewn mine
shaft, boots scraping on stone. Amber light from crystallized ley
veins pulses in the walls.)*

### 1a. Upper Mine — First Dead

*(Floor 1. The tunnel opens into a wider passage. Three miners sit
slumped against the far wall. No blood. No wounds. Their faces are
frozen in expressions of complete despair.)*

**EDREN** : Hold.

*(Edren kneels beside the nearest miner. Checks for a pulse. Nothing.)*

**EDREN** : No marks. No signs of a fight. They just... stopped.

**CAEL** : They didn't die fighting. They died feeling something.

*(Edren stands. Looks further down the corridor.)*

**EDREN** : There'll be more.

**CAEL** : You say that like you've seen it before.

**EDREN** : I haven't. That's what worries me.

### 1b. Lower Mine — The Letter

<!-- Cross-ref: dungeons-world.md § 1 Ember Vein Floor 2 -->

*(Floor 2. Six more miners lie in the corridors. One clutches a
half-written letter. Another's hand stretches toward the stairs,
fingers clawed at stone — crawling back toward the surface.)*

**CAEL** : This one was writing home.

*(Cael [head_down]. He reads the letter in silence, then sets it
back.)*

**CAEL** : He didn't finish.

**EDREN** : They were trying to get out.

**CAEL** : Some of them. The ones by the Pendulum chamber weren't
trying to get anywhere. They just sat down and let it happen.

*(Edren notices a miner's journal on the ground. Picks it up.)*

**EDREN** : "The growths drink deep — even stale water wakes them
for a spell." Miners were using water on the ley crystals.

*(Edren pockets a Mine Water Vial from beside the journal.)*

**EDREN** : Could be useful.

### 1c. Ancient Ruin — The Carvings

<!-- Cross-ref: dungeons-world.md § 1 Ember Vein Floor 3 -->

*(Floor 3. The rough mine walls give way to smooth geometric stone.
The architecture changes completely — polished dark blocks fitted
without mortar, lines precise, inhuman. Ley energy pulses in
synchronized patterns along carved channels. A low vibration hums
beneath hearing.)*

**EDREN** : This wasn't built by miners.

**CAEL** : This wasn't built by anyone we'd recognize.

*(They approach a wall covered in geometric murals. A pendulum-like
shape at the center, surrounded by kneeling figures.)*

**CAEL** : Look at this. Whatever they built down here, that
object was at the center of it.

**EDREN** : The figures aren't worshipping. Look at their posture.

**CAEL** : They're bowing under weight. Like they're holding
something up.

*(Silence. The hum intensifies slightly as they move deeper.)*

### 1d. The Pendulum Chamber

<!-- Cross-ref: dungeons-world.md § 1 Ember Vein Floor 4 -->

*(Floor 4. A perfect dome ceiling. Concentric ring patterns carved
into the floor radiate from a stone pedestal at the center. Amber
light, brighter here than anywhere else. The air vibrates.)*

*(Seven dead miners sit in a circle around the pedestal. Their faces
show absolute hopelessness — not pain, not fear, but the total
absence of any reason to continue. On the pedestal: the Pendulum.
Small. Brass. Its needle does not move.)*

**CAEL** : Seven of them. Arranged like the mural.

**EDREN** : They arranged themselves. Look — no drag marks.

**CAEL** : They walked in, sat down, and gave up.

*(Long pause. The hum fills the silence.)*

**EDREN** : We can't leave it here. If the Compact finds it—

*(Edren reaches for the Pendulum.)*

**CAEL** : Don't.

*(Cael [bubble_exclaim]. He grabs Edren's arm.)*

**CAEL** : Something about this place. I can feel it.

**EDREN** : We don't have a choice. We report to Valdris.
Whatever this is, the court needs to know.

*(Edren picks up the Pendulum. The needle twitches once. The room
shudders — dust falls from the dome, ley channels flare bright,
then dim. [SFX: ley_surge])*

**CAEL** : Something heard that.

*(Silence. Then, distantly, the sound of boots above them. Many
boots.)*

**EDREN** : Compact soldiers. Move.

### 1e. Vein Guardian

<!-- Cross-ref: bestiary/bosses.md § Vein Guardian -->

*(The corridor ahead seals with crystalline growth. A construct
assembles from the walls — geometric, angular, twice human height,
glowing ember-orange. The Vein Guardian.)*

**EDREN** : It's protecting the chamber. Or keeping us in.

**CAEL** : Same result either way. Ready?

*(Boss fight: Vein Guardian. Lv 12, HP 6,000. Two phases.
Tutorial fight — teaches defend timing and healing economy.)*

*(After the Vein Guardian shatters, the crystalline seal crumbles.
A hidden passage slopes upward behind it — rougher stone, older
than the mine.)*

**EDREN** : This way. The soldiers are above us, not ahead.

**CAEL** : You hope.

## Scene 2: The Stranger at the Mine

<!-- Scene: vaelith_ember_vein | Tier: 1 | Trigger: vaelith_ember_vein (flag 2) -->
<!-- Location: Ember Vein exterior, cliff-face near Ironmouth | Party: Edren, Cael -->
<!-- Variants: none -->
<!-- Cross-ref: outline.md § Act I The Stranger at the Mine, npcs.md § Vaelith -->

*(The hidden passage opens onto a cliff-face. Night air. Stars.
The mine is below them, lanterns of Compact soldiers visible at
the entrance. Edren and Cael emerge, breathing hard.)*

*(A figure leans against a broken stone column nearby, grey cloak
pulled loose in the night air. They have been waiting. Not hiding.
Waiting.)*

**VAELITH** : Ah. You made it out. I was beginning to wonder.

*(Edren [bubble_exclaim]. He draws his sword. Cael tenses.)*

**VAELITH** : Please. If I wanted to stop you, I wouldn't have
waited up here in the cold.

*(Vaelith does not reach for a weapon. Does not move from the
column. Smiles.)*

**VAELITH** : My name is Vaelith. I study old places — ruins,
mostly. I've been watching this mine for some time. The Compact's
excavation was... ambitious, if graceless.

**EDREN** : What do you want?

**VAELITH** : May I see what you found?

**EDREN** : No.

*(Vaelith [nod]. Tilts head slightly — academic disappointment,
not anger.)*

**VAELITH** : Of course. I wouldn't trust a stranger on a cliff
at night either.

*(Vaelith studies the Pendulum from a polite distance. Eyes narrow
briefly, then relax.)*

**VAELITH** : What a fragile little thing it is to build hope
around.

*(Beat.)*

**EDREN** : We're taking it to Valdris.

**VAELITH** : Naturally. Safe journey, then. The trail west is
clear — the soldiers are focused on the mine entrance.

*(Vaelith bows — a genuine, courteous bow — and walks back toward
the darkness without haste. No threat made. No threat needed.)*

*(Edren and Cael watch until the grey cloak vanishes.)*

**CAEL** : Who was that?

**EDREN** : I don't know. And I don't like it.

**CAEL** : He knew we'd come out this way.

**EDREN** : I know.

*(They move on into the night.)*

## Scene 3: Escape from Ironmouth — Lira and Sable

<!-- Scene: ironmouth_escape | Tier: 2 | Trigger: pendulum_discovered (flag 1) -->
<!-- Location: Ironmouth docks, cliff trail | Party: Edren, Cael → adds Lira, Sable -->
<!-- Variants: none -->
<!-- Cross-ref: outline.md § Act I Party Assembly, locations.md § Ironmouth -->

*(Dawn. The cliff trail descends toward Ironmouth's dock district.
The mining outpost is stirring — Compact soldiers moving in
organized sweeps, searching the perimeter. Edren and Cael keep
low, moving along the cliff edge.)*

*(A woman crouches behind a stack of Forgewright supply crates,
watching the same soldiers. She wears a Carradan fieldworker's
coat — but her hands are an engineer's, and the satchel at her
side holds documents, not tools. This is Lira.)*

**LIRA** : Stop. Don't move.

*(Edren and Cael freeze. Lira doesn't look at them — she's
counting soldiers.)*

**LIRA** : Patrol passes in forty seconds. You came from the
mine?

**EDREN** : Who are you?

**LIRA** : Someone who's been tracking what the Compact pulled
out of that ruin for three months. My name is Lira. I'm a
Forgewright — or I was, until I read the wrong documents.

**CAEL** : You defected.

**LIRA** : I found references to something called "Project
Pendulum" in a classified engineering report. The Compact doesn't
classify engineering reports. That was enough.

*(The patrol passes below them. Lira counts under her breath.)*

**LIRA** : Now. This way.

*(They move along the dock's upper walkway. Below, two soldiers
detach from the sweep and head toward the cliff stairs.)*

**EDREN** : We're cut off.

*(A crate slides into the soldiers' path from behind a pillar. A
young woman steps out — dark clothes, quick eyes, a grin that
suggests she's been having fun with this.)*

**SABLE** : Sorry, gentlemen. Inventory check. Very official.
Come back tomorrow.

*(The soldiers stumble. Sable is already moving — she grabs a
mooring rope and swings to the upper walkway, landing beside the
party with a thud.)*

**SABLE** : Friends of yours, or am I interrupting something
private?

**EDREN** : We're leaving. Now.

**SABLE** : Perfect. I was just leaving too. I was definitely
not in the middle of relieving the quartermaster's office of
anything valuable.

*(She holds up a supply manifest, then tucks it away.)*

**SABLE** : I'm Sable. And unless one of you has a boat, we're
all going through the Wilds.

*(More soldiers converge on the docks. The cliff trail north is
still clear.)*

**LIRA** : She's right. The forest is the only way.

**EDREN** : Then we move. Stay together.

*(The four of them slip into the treeline as Ironmouth's alarm
bells begin to ring. [SFX: alarm_bells])*

## Scene 4: The Dawn March

<!-- Scene: dawn_march | Tier: 2 | Trigger: carradan_ambush_survived (flag 3) -->
<!-- Location: Trail from Ironmouth toward Thornmere Wilds | Party: Edren, Cael, Lira, Sable -->
<!-- Variants: none -->
<!-- Cross-ref: outline.md § Act I The Dawn March, events.md flag 39 -->

*(Dawn breaks over the Wilds border. A narrow trail winds through
old-growth forest, morning light filtering through the canopy.
Forward-only walk — no encounters. Music shifts to something
quieter, reflective.)*

*(Edren walks ahead. Cael beside him. Lira and Sable trail a few
paces behind, visible but separate.)*

**CAEL** : Those miners. They just stopped. No fight, no wounds.
They gave up.

**EDREN** : We'll let the court decide what it is.

**CAEL** : And the stranger? You're not concerned?

**EDREN** : I'm concerned about all of it.

*(The trail widens. The valley opens below — rolling foothills
green and gold in morning light, distant pale towers of Valdris
Crown catching the sun.)*

*(Beat of silence. They walk.)*

**CAEL** : I keep thinking about their faces.

*(Edren says nothing. He walks. Behind them, Lira scans the
treeline. Sable twists a coin between her fingers, watching
everything.)*

*(The trail descends into deeper forest. The canopy closes
overhead. A game title card appears:)*

*(Center screen, white text on black:)*

## **PENDULUM OF DESPAIR**

*(Credits overlay the walk. Character names. "A game by..." The
party continues forward. The world opens ahead of them.
[SFX: title_reveal])*

*(Flag `opening_credits_seen` (39) set. Tutorial complete.)*

## Scene 5: Thornmere Wilds — Torren

<!-- Scene: torren_encounter | Tier: 3 | Trigger: entering Roothollow zone -->
<!-- Location: Wildwood Trail → Roothollow | Party: Edren, Cael, Lira, Sable → adds Torren -->
<!-- Variants: none -->
<!-- Cross-ref: outline.md § Act I Party Assembly, locations.md § Roothollow, npcs.md § Vessa/Torren -->

### 5a. The Wildwood Trail

*(Deep forest. Ancient trees so wide their roots form walls.
Bioluminescent moss casts blue-green light on the undergrowth.
The trail narrows to a path marked by carved wooden totems —
faces worn smooth by weather, old script spiraling down their
posts.)*

*(A figure steps onto the trail ahead of them. Older, weathered
hands, carrying a staff of living wood. He has been tracking them
for some time. This is Torren.)*

**TORREN** : Stop there.

*(The party halts. Torren's eyes move from face to face —
assessment, not hostility. His gaze settles on the satchel where
Edren carries the Pendulum.)*

**TORREN** : You're carrying something that doesn't belong in
these woods.

**EDREN** : We're passing through. We mean no—

**TORREN** : I know what you mean. I also know what you're
carrying. The totems told me before you crossed the ridge.

**SABLE** : The... totems told you.

**TORREN** : They shake when that thing gets close. They've been
shaking since dawn.

*(He approaches slowly. Studies the party. [nod] — a decision
made.)*

**TORREN** : My name is Torren. I speak for the spirits of these
woods — what's left of them. Where are you taking it?

**EDREN** : Valdris. We're reporting to the crown.

**TORREN** : The crown doesn't know what this is. Neither do you.

**LIRA** : And you do?

**TORREN** : No. But I know someone who might. A woman lives
deep in the forest. Maren. She was a court mage before she was
an exile. If anyone understands what you've found, it's her.

**CAEL** : Why would an exile help us?

**TORREN** : Because she chose exile to study things like this.
Come. I'll take you to her. But we stop at Roothollow first —
my people deserve to know what's passing through their land.

### 5b. Roothollow — Vessa's Warning

<!-- Cross-ref: locations.md § Roothollow, npcs.md § Vessa -->

*(Roothollow. The settlement is built inside the root system of
a tree so ancient its canopy forms its own world. Bioluminescent
moss lines every surface — cyan, teal, soft purple from the
spirit-totems at intersections. The chambers are grown, not
built. The air is warm, alive.)*

*(Vessa waits at the entrance to the central chamber. She is old.
She has kept her people safe for decades. She can feel that
safety crumbling.)*

**VESSA** : Torren. I felt you coming. I felt what they carry.

*(She looks past Torren to Edren. Her expression is not hostile —
it is the face of a woman measuring a threat she cannot fight.)*

**VESSA** : That thing you carry — the totems know it. They're
shaking. Take it from my home before dawn, or I will put it in
the river myself.

**EDREN** : We don't intend to stay. Torren says there's someone
who can—

**VESSA** : Maren. Yes. She'll understand what you've found. I
understand enough to know it shouldn't be here.

*(She turns to Torren.)*

**VESSA** : Go with them. Make sure that thing reaches someone
who can contain it — not use it. Not study it. Contain it.

*(Torren [nod].)*

**TORREN** : I will.

**VESSA** : And Torren — when you come back, bring me something
that isn't dread. I'm tired of dread.

*(Torren allows himself a small smile.)*

*(Flag `torren_joined` (4) set. Torren joins the party.)*

## Scene 6: Maren's Warning

<!-- Scene: maren_warning | Tier: 1+2 | Trigger: party reaches Maren's Refuge -->
<!-- Location: Maren's Refuge, deep Thornmere Wilds | Party: Edren, Cael, Lira, Sable, Torren → adds Maren -->
<!-- Variants: none -->
<!-- Cross-ref: outline.md § Act I The First Warning, locations.md § Maren's Refuge, npcs.md § Maren -->

### 6a. Arrival

*(Deep forest. No sunlight reaches the floor. A stone cottage
sits half-swallowed by the woods — moss covers the walls, roots
push through the floorboards, shelves of books visible through
dusty windows. It should look abandoned. The door is unlocked.
The kettle is warm.)*

*(The party enters. The interior is larger than the exterior
suggests — shelves crammed with texts from a dozen civilizations,
ley crystals mounted on iron frames, spirit creatures drifting
near the ceiling like luminous moths.)*

*(Maren sits at a desk in the back of the room. She is already
reading. She does not look up immediately.)*

**MAREN** : Torren sent word through the spirits. Faster than
walking, though less polite.

*(She sets her book down. Stands. Studies the party — not their
faces, but the satchel.)*

**MAREN** : Show me.

*(Edren places the Pendulum on the desk. Maren leans close. Does
not touch it. Her eyes narrow.)*

### 6b. Examination

*(Maren circles the desk. She holds a crystal lens to her eye,
peers at the Pendulum from several angles. The spirit creatures
drift away from it. The room's warmth drops slightly.)*

**MAREN** : Where did you find it?

**EDREN** : Beneath an Ironmouth mine. A pre-civilization ruin.
There were dead miners — no wounds. Their faces were—

**MAREN** : Frozen in despair. Yes. I've read about this.

*(She sets the lens down. Steps back.)*

**MAREN** : This thing has no power. It's not a weapon, not a
source, not a tool.

**LIRA** : Then what is it?

*(Maren [bubble_ellipsis]. A pause — she is choosing her words
with care.)*

**MAREN** : It's a door. And something on the other side knows
you opened it.

*(Silence. The spirit creatures have withdrawn entirely. The
Pendulum sits on the desk, small, brass, its needle still.)*

**CAEL** : A door to what?

**MAREN** : I don't know everything. I know this: the miners
didn't die from the Pendulum. They died from what's behind it.
Despair so deep it replaces the will to live. They sat down and
the world emptied out of them.

**TORREN** : The spirits call it the Grey. Old word. Older than
the tribes.

**MAREN** : Different names in different ages. The Pallor. The
Grey. The Weight. It doesn't matter what you call it. It's been
on the other side of that door for a very long time, and now it
knows someone touched the handle.

### 6c. The Decision

**EDREN** : Can it be destroyed?

**MAREN** : I don't know. I know it can't be left here. The ley
line beneath this cottage is already dimming — I can feel it
responding to the Pendulum's presence. Wherever you take it, the
world around it will start to fade.

**LIRA** : Then we take it to people who can study it properly.
Valdris has resources — containment, ley researchers, the court
mages.

**MAREN** : The court mages will want to use it. The king will
want it as leverage against the Compact. And the thing behind
the door will let them think they're in control right up until
they aren't.

**SABLE** : So we can't leave it, can't destroy it, and can't
give it to anyone. Wonderful.

**MAREN** : We can take it to people who understand the danger.
And I can be one of those people.

*(She begins pulling books from shelves, packing a travel bag
with practiced efficiency.)*

**MAREN** : I left the court because they wouldn't listen. Maybe
now they'll have to.

**TORREN** : You're coming with us?

**MAREN** : I've spent years studying this in isolation. If that
door is opening, isolation isn't enough anymore.

*(Maren [nod]. She shoulders her bag.)*

**MAREN** : To Valdris, then. And pray they listen better than
they did last time.

*(Flag `maren_warning` (5) set. Maren joins the party.)*

## Scene 7: The Capital

<!-- Scene: arrival_at_valdris | Tier: 2+3 | Trigger: pendulum_to_capital (flag 6) -->
<!-- Location: Valdris Crown | Party: Edren, Cael, Lira, Sable, Torren, Maren -->
<!-- Variants: none -->
<!-- Cross-ref: outline.md § Act I Arrival, locations.md § Valdris Crown, npcs.md § King Aldren / Aldis / Cordwyn / Haren -->

### 7a. The Gates

*(Valdris Crown. The capital rises from a natural plateau — pale
limestone terraces descending from the Royal Keep at the summit.
Seven towers mark ley line positions, glowing faintly gold in the
afternoon light. The city is beautiful. It is also, if you look
closely, beginning to fray — a ley-lamp flickers on the gate
tower, a market stall sits empty where there should be three.)*

*(The party approaches the main gate. Guards recognize Edren.)*

**GATE GUARD** : Commander Edren. You've been expected. The king
received word from Thornwatch.

**EDREN** : Commander Halda sent ahead?

**GATE GUARD** : A rider, two days ago. The king's been asking
for you since.

*(The guard's eyes flick to the rest of the party — a Forgewright,
a Wilds ranger, a woman who looks like she hasn't left a library
in years, and a young woman who is very carefully not making eye
contact.)*

**GATE GUARD** : Your... company will need to register at the
inner gate.

**SABLE** : Of course. I love registering.

### 7b. The Throne Hall

<!-- Cross-ref: npcs.md § King Aldren, npcs.md § Lord Chancellor Haren -->

*(The Royal Keep. The throne hall is smaller than expected — built
for function, not grandeur. Stone columns, banner tapestries
showing the seven towers, ley-crystal sconces casting warm light.
King Aldren sits on a plain stone chair at the far end. He is
older than he should be. Lord Chancellor Haren stands to his
left.)*

**KING ALDREN** : Edren. You look like you've walked through a
war.

**EDREN** : Nearly, Your Majesty. We found something in an
Ironmouth mine that the Compact was excavating. A pre-civilization
artifact.

*(Edren places the Pendulum on the table before the throne. It
sits there, small and inert.)*

**KING ALDREN** : This is what Halda's rider described? It
doesn't look like much.

**MAREN** : It isn't much. That's the danger, Your Majesty. It's
a conduit — a focal point for something beyond it. The miners who
found it are dead. Not from violence. From despair.

**HAREN** : Maren. You've been away from court for some time.

**MAREN** : Not by choice, Chancellor. I left because my warnings
were ignored. I'm here because this time, ignoring them will cost
more than my career.

*(Aldren studies the Pendulum. Then studies Maren. Then Edren.)*

**KING ALDREN** : Every morning I ask the court mages for a
report on the ley lines. Every morning, the number is lower.
I've stopped asking what it means.

*(He stands. Approaches the table.)*

**KING ALDREN** : You brought this to me for safekeeping. I
wonder if you understand — there is nothing safe left in Valdris.

**EDREN** : Safer here than in Compact hands.

**KING ALDREN** : Perhaps. Maren — I want your assessment in
writing by morning. Cael, you studied ley resonance at the
academy. I'm assigning you to work with Maren on this.

**CAEL** : Of course, Your Majesty.

*(Cael [nod]. His agreement is immediate, willing. No hesitation.
This is the moment the trap closes — the Pallor's mark answers
the king's request before Cael can think twice.)*

**KING ALDREN** : The rest of you — welcome to Valdris. Such as
it is. Find quarters in the Keep and rest. I expect we'll need
you soon.

### 7c. The Court

*(The throne hall empties. The party disperses into the Keep. This
is a free-roam section — the player can speak to key NPCs before
the act ends.)*

#### Scholar Aldis (Royal Library)

<!-- Cross-ref: npcs.md § Scholar Aldis -->

**ALDIS** : Commander Edren. I've been cataloguing the ley line
decline for three years now. Twelve percent capacity loss in the
last year alone. I presented these findings to the court. They
asked me if I was certain about the margin of error.

*(He gestures to stacks of careful notes.)*

**ALDIS** : If your artifact is related to the ley line failures,
I'd like to compare my readings. The resonance patterns may
correlate. I'll work with Cael — he has a mind for this kind of
analysis.

#### Dame Cordwyn (Knight's Barracks)

<!-- Cross-ref: npcs.md § Dame Cordwyn -->

**CORDWYN** : Edren. Good to see you in one piece.

*(She clasps his arm — a soldier's greeting.)*

**CORDWYN** : Heard you pulled something out of an old mine that
has the whole court whispering. The Chancellor's already trying
to figure out how to use it as a bargaining chip with the
Compact.

**EDREN** : It's not a weapon.

**CORDWYN** : Everything's a weapon to a politician. Just make
sure Cael doesn't lose himself in that research. He's brilliant,
but he carries more than he lets on.

*(A beat. Cordwyn's expression shifts — something unsaid.)*

**CORDWYN** : Keep an eye on him. That's not an order. It's a
request.

#### Renn (Lower Ward Tavern)

<!-- Cross-ref: npcs.md § Renn -->

*(Sable peels off from the group toward the Lower Ward. She
enters a dockside tavern with practiced familiarity.)*

**RENN** : Sable. You're supposed to be dead in a ditch
somewhere.

**SABLE** : I get that a lot.

**RENN** : Sit down. You look like you've been running from
something expensive.

**SABLE** : Running toward something, actually. I need
information.

**RENN** : I don't sell secrets, love. I sell certainty. You
want to know something for sure? That costs extra.

**SABLE** : What do you know about the Compact's mining
operations in the southern Wilds?

**RENN** : I know they found something they weren't expecting.
I know three courier birds went to Corrund in one day. And I
know the price of Forgewright steel just dropped, which means
the generals are spending money on something other than weapons.

*(She slides a drink across the bar.)*

**RENN** : Whatever you've gotten yourself into, Sable — it's
bigger than a mine.

**SABLE** : Yeah. I'm starting to figure that out.

### 7d. Evening — Cael and the Pendulum

<!-- Cross-ref: events.md flag 6, characters.md § Cael -->

*(Night. The Pendulum sits in a sealed chamber in the lower Keep,
surrounded by containment wards that glow faintly blue. Cael
stands before it, alone. His hand hovers near the glass case.
He does not touch it.)*

*(His reflection stares back at him in the glass. For a single
frame — a flicker so brief a player might miss it — his
reflection's eyes are grey.)*

*(He withdraws his hand. Steps back. Leaves the chamber.)*

*(The Pendulum's needle shifts. One degree. Then stops.)*

*(Flag `pendulum_to_capital` (6) set. Act I ends.)*
