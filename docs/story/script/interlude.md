# Interlude: The Unraveling

> The world broke. The party scattered. One person refused to stop
> looking. Sable — the thief, the liar, the one with no particular
> talent except showing up — tracks down every party member across a
> continent that's falling apart. The player controls Sable for the
> first and only time. The reunion order is player-chosen, and each
> reunion changes the emotional texture of the ones that follow.

## Scene 21: Sable Wakes

<!-- Scene: sable_aftermath | Tier: 2 | Trigger: ley_line_rupture (flag 21) -->
<!-- Location: Valdris Crown, Lower Ward | Party: Sable (solo) -->
<!-- Variants: none -->
<!-- Cross-ref: outline.md § Interlude The Party Scatters, locations.md § Valdris Crown -->

*(Weeks later. The Lower Ward of Valdris Crown. The city is
broken — makeshift barricades divide neighborhoods, buildings
bear scorch marks from the siege, ley-lamps are dark. Grey dust
settles in corners. A few vendors hawk goods at inflated prices.
Some citizens stand motionless in doorways, staring at nothing.)*

*(Sable sits on a rooftop, legs dangling over the edge. She is
watching the city below. She has been watching for a while.)*

**SABLE** : Right. So. Everything's terrible. Just... establishing
that baseline.

*(She stands. Stretches. The humor doesn't land, and she knows
it.)*

**SABLE** : Edren's gone. Lira's gone. Torren's back in the
Wilds. Maren left a note and vanished. Cael is... Cael.

*(She looks out across the broken skyline.)*

**SABLE** : And I'm still here. The thief with no particular
talents except being too stubborn to leave.

*(She climbs down from the roof, drops to street level.)*

**SABLE** : Guess that'll have to be enough.

*(She reaches the street. A vendor at a half-collapsed stall
sells bread at triple price. A woman stands in a doorway,
staring at nothing. A ley-lamp on the corner flickers twice
and goes dark.)*

*(Sable walks past them all. She's not a hero. She's not a
knight. She's the one who's still moving when everyone else
has sat down. That has to count for something.)*

## Scene 22: Renn's Intelligence

<!-- Scene: renn_gathering | Tier: 3 | Trigger: sable_aftermath complete -->
<!-- Location: The Anchor and Oar tavern, Valdris Lower Ward | Party: Sable (solo) -->
<!-- Variants: none -->
<!-- Cross-ref: npcs.md § Renn, locations.md § Valdris Crown -->

*(The Anchor and Oar. Half the stools are empty. The ones that
aren't hold people drinking with the focus of those who have
nothing else to do. Renn stands behind the bar, polishing a glass
that's already clean.)*

**RENN** : Sable. You look like you've been sleeping on rooftops
again.

**SABLE** : Rooftops are fine. It's the ground that's the problem.

**RENN** : Half my regulars are dead or gone. The other half drink
twice as much. Business has never been worse and never been
better.

*(She sets down the glass.)*

**RENN** : You're not here for a drink.

**SABLE** : I need to find them. All of them. Edren, Lira, Torren,
Maren.

**RENN** : That's four people scattered across a continent that's
falling apart. Even for you, that's ambitious.

**SABLE** : Renn. Please.

*(The humor drops. Sable's voice is raw. Renn studies her face
for a moment.)*

**RENN** : Edren's at Highcairn. The monastery in the highlands.
Cordwyn got word from a rider three days ago — he's alive but
he's not talking.

**SABLE** : And the others?

**RENN** : Lira went back to the Compact. Infiltrated under a
false name. Last I heard, she was in Corrund chasing something
through the Consortium's archives. That was two weeks ago.

**SABLE** : Torren?

**RENN** : Back in the Wilds. The spirit-speakers are saying the
whole forest is dying. Torren's trying to hold it together.

**SABLE** : Maren?

**RENN** : Gone. Left a note at Cordwyn's office: "I've gone to
find what came before the Pendulum." Nobody knows where that
means.

*(She slides a folded map across the bar.)*

**RENN** : Start with whoever you can reach. Come back when you
have news. I'll keep my ears open.

**SABLE** : What do I owe you?

**RENN** : Come back alive. That's the price.

*(Four markers appear on the world map. Player chooses reunion
order.)*

## Scene 23: Finding Edren

<!-- Scene: finding_edren | Tier: 2+3 | Trigger: player travels to Highcairn -->
<!-- Location: Highcairn Monastery | Party: Sable + previously found members -->
<!-- Variants: reunion_order position affects emotional tone -->
<!-- Cross-ref: outline.md § Interlude Finding Edren, npcs.md § Father Aldous, locations.md § Highcairn -->

### 23a. The Monastery

*(Highcairn Monastery. Mountain pass. The building sits on a
ledge overlooking a valley gone grey — fog that doesn't move,
forests that look like charcoal sketches. Grey frost covers the
prayer stones in the courtyard. The monks move slowly, hollow-eyed.
One sits facing a wall and does not respond when Sable passes.)*

*(Father Aldous meets them at the entrance. Old, broad-shouldered,
hands that once held a sword and now hold bread dough.)*

**ALDOUS** : You're looking for the knight.

**SABLE** : Edren. Is he—

**ALDOUS** : Alive. Sitting. That's about all I can tell you.

*(He leads them into the great hall. A fire burns in the hearth —
the flame looks wrong, more white than orange.)*

**ALDOUS** : I was a knight once. Then I was a coward. Then I was
a monk. Now I'm just a man who makes bread and prays. The order
matters less than you think.

### 23b. Edren

*(The great hall. Edren sits on a bench near the hearth. He is
not injured. He is not praying. He is sitting with his hands on
his knees, staring at the floor. His armor is piled in a corner,
unbuckled.)*

**SABLE** : Edren.

*(He looks up. Recognition. No relief.)*

**EDREN** : Sable. You shouldn't have come.

**SABLE** : Yeah, well. Nobody should do a lot of things.

*(She sits beside him. Not close. Close enough.)*

**EDREN** : I didn't see it. Months of it — the nightmares, the
isolation, the way he stopped looking people in the eye — and I
didn't see it.

**SABLE** : None of us did.

**EDREN** : I'm his commanding officer. His friend. I should have—

**SABLE** : Should have what? Read his mind? Outthought something
that's been breaking people since before the towers were built?

*(Edren says nothing.)*

**SABLE** : Edren. The world is falling apart. The Wilds are
dying. The Compact is eating itself. Valdris doesn't have a king
anymore. And you're sitting in a monastery feeling sorry about
something that wasn't your fault.

**EDREN** : I can't—

**SABLE** : You can. I know you can, because I'm here, and I'm
nobody. I'm a thief from the Caldera undercity with no money, no
titles, and no plan. And I'm still moving. So if I can do it, you
damn well can.

*(Silence. The fire crackles.)*

*(Aldous's voice from behind them.)*

**ALDOUS** : She's right, you know. You came here to hide, son.
That's allowed. But this place isn't for hiding. It's for
remembering what you're hiding from.

### 23c. The Pallor Manifestation

*(A rumble. The grey frost on the prayer stones spreads suddenly —
climbing the walls, creeping across the floor. The monks
stagger. [SFX: pallor_surge])*

**ALDOUS** : That's new.

*(Grey shapes coalesce from the frost — Pallor manifestations,
hollow and grinding. They move toward the monastery hall.)*

**SABLE** : Edren.

*(She holds out his sword. She brought it.)*

*(Edren stares at it. Stares at the shapes coming through the
doorway. Stares at Sable.)*

*(He takes the sword.)*

*(Edren [nod]. Not healed. Not ready. But people need him.)*

**EDREN** : Stay behind me.

<!-- Cross-ref: dungeons-world.md § Highcairn Monastery (Pallor encounter) -->

*(Battle: Pallor Manifestation. After victory, the frost recedes.
The monastery stabilizes.)*

### 23d. Reunion

*(After the battle. Edren stands in the courtyard, breathing
hard. The grey frost is melting. The monks emerge from hiding.)*

**EDREN** : Where are the others?

**SABLE** : Scattered. I'm collecting. You're first.

(If Edren is NOT first reunion and `lira_found` set.)
**SABLE** : Scattered. Lira's with me. She infiltrated the
Compact — found Cael's trail.

**EDREN** : She went back to the Compact? Alone?

**SABLE** : She's not alone anymore. Neither are you.

(If Edren is NOT first reunion and `torren_found` set.)
**SABLE** : Torren's alive. The Wilds nearly killed him — he
was holding back the corruption by himself. We stabilized the
nexus.

**EDREN** : The nexus held?

**SABLE** : First clean ley energy since the rupture. The
Pallor can be pushed back. That's what matters.

(If Edren is NOT first reunion and `maren_found` set.)
**SABLE** : I found Maren. She was in a ruin older than
civilization. She found the pattern — the Pallor has done this
before.

**EDREN** : How many times?

**SABLE** : Enough times that someone built a library to
track it.

**EDREN** : Then we keep moving. I wasted enough time sitting.

*(He buckles his armor. The joints creak — he's been still too
long.)*

*(Aldous watches from the doorway.)*

**ALDOUS** : Go well, knight. And next time you visit, bring
something to drink. The wine here is terrible.

*(Flag `edren_found` (22) set. Edren rejoins the party.)*

## Scene 24: Finding Lira

<!-- Scene: finding_lira | Tier: 2+3 | Trigger: player travels to Corrund -->
<!-- Location: Corrund → Ironmark Citadel | Party: Sable + previously found members -->
<!-- Variants: reunion_order position; party_has checks for infiltration dialogue -->
<!-- Cross-ref: outline.md § Interlude Finding Lira, npcs.md § Tash/Brant/Veld/Kole, locations.md § Corrund -->

### 24a. Corrund Undercity

*(Corrund. The industrial city is failing — factory smoke thinner,
canal water grey-filmed, Forgewright engines grinding with wrong
sounds. Districts have lost power. Mechanical streetcars sit
abandoned mid-route.)*

*(Sable leads the party through the canal district to a hidden
workshop beneath a condemned foundry. Tash — black market dealer,
Sable's old contact — waits at the entrance.)*

**TASH** : Sable. You still owe me for that Ashport job.

**SABLE** : I owe a lot of people. You're near the top of a very
long list.

**TASH** : Your engineer friend is in the upper city. Hidden
workshop off the tertiary canal. She's been there two weeks, nose
buried in stolen documents. Doesn't eat much.

**SABLE** : That sounds like Lira.

**TASH** : I got you papers. Forged Compact engineering credentials.
Good enough for checkpoints, not good enough for a thorough search.
Don't get thoroughly searched.

### 24b. Lira's Workshop

*(A hidden room above a condemned canal house. Lira sits at a
workbench covered in stolen Consortium documents, engineering
schematics, and maps with circles and arrows drawn in red ink.
She doesn't look up when the door opens.)*

**LIRA** : Close the door. I'm close.

**SABLE** : Close to what?

*(Lira turns. Relief, then embarrassment at the relief, then a
mask of focus.)*

**LIRA** : Sable. You found me.

**SABLE** : I found everyone. That's my thing now, apparently.
Finding people who don't want to be found.

**LIRA** : I want to be found. I just didn't want to stop working
long enough to be findable.

*(She gestures at the documents.)*

**LIRA** : Cael's trail. He's not hiding — he went straight to
the Convergence. The Compact has surveillance records. Three
separate patrols spotted him crossing the Wastes. He's building
something at the center of it.

(If `edren_found` set.)
*(Edren steps forward. Studies the map.)*

**EDREN** : How long ago?

**LIRA** : Weeks. He's had time.

(If `torren_found` set.)
**TORREN** : The spirits won't go near the Convergence. Whatever
he's building, it's drawing the Pallor to it.

**LIRA** : There's a problem. The map to the Convergence — the
detailed route, the passes through the Wastes — it's locked in
Ironmark Citadel. Kole has it.

**SABLE** : Kole. The general?

**LIRA** : The general who decided that if the ley lines won't
cooperate, Pallor energy makes a fine substitute. He's running
the Citadel on it. His soldiers are... different now.

### 24c. Ironmark Citadel — Brant

*(The Citadel's service entrance. Commissar Brant waits in the
shadows — gaunt, unable to meet their eyes. He opens a rear gate
with a key that shakes in his hand.)*

**BRANT** : I didn't open that gate for you. I opened it for me.
There's a difference. Don't thank me.

**SABLE** : Wasn't planning on it.

**BRANT** : Kole's on the command level. The soldiers between you
and him — they're not soldiers anymore. They follow orders because
the Pallor energy strips out everything that would make them
question.

*(He backs away into the darkness.)*

**BRANT** : I'll be here when it's done. Or I won't. Either way.

### 24d. Ironmark Citadel — Ansa Veld

*(The lower cells. Behind a reinforced door, Lieutenant Ansa Veld
sits on a stone bench. She is thinner than she should be. Her
eyes are sharp.)*

**VELD** : He said it was a choice. It was. I chose no. Then I
learned what "no" costs in the new Compact.

*(She stands. Looks at the party.)*

**VELD** : There were thirty of us who refused. I count sixteen
now. The others didn't die. They just stopped being themselves.

**SABLE** : We're taking down Kole. What can you tell us?

**VELD** : His command chamber is on the top level. The Pallor
energy flows from a conduit he installed — shut that down and his
hold on the soldiers breaks. The map you want is in the war room,
one floor below.

### 24e. Kole

<!-- Cross-ref: bestiary/bosses.md § General Vassar Kole -->

*(The command chamber. General Vassar Kole stands at a strategic
table, grey energy pulsing from conduits in the walls. He is calm.
Not ranting, not monologuing — calm.)*

**KOLE** : You think I was corrupted? I was clarified. The ley
lines failed. The Consortium failed. The treaties, the alliances,
the careful politics — all of it failed. The only thing that
doesn't fail is certainty. And the Pallor is very, very certain.

**LIRA** : Certainty isn't the same as truth.

**KOLE** : No. It's better. Truth changes. Certainty endures.

*(Boss fight: General Vassar Kole. Lv 28, 30,000 HP. 2 phases.)*

*(After Kole falls, the conduits shatter. The Pallor energy in
the Citadel dissipates. Soldiers collapse — some wake confused,
some don't wake at all.)*

### 24f. Reunion

*(The war room. Lira spreads the recovered map across the table.
The route to the Convergence — through the Pallor Wastes, past
terrain no patrol has survived.)*

**LIRA** : This is it. The route to Cael.

*(She stares at the map. Her finger traces the path.)*

**LIRA** : He went alone. Through all of this. Alone.

*(A pause.)*

(If `edren_found` set.)
**EDREN** : He's not alone anymore.

(If Edren NOT found.)
**SABLE** : Neither are we. Come on. More people to find.

*(Flag `lira_found` (23) set. Lira rejoins the party.)*

## Scene 25: Finding Torren

<!-- Scene: finding_torren | Tier: 2+3 | Trigger: player travels to Roothollow -->
<!-- Location: Thornmere Wilds → Roothollow → Ley Nexus | Party: Sable + previously found members -->
<!-- Variants: reunion_order position; stag_bonded affects post-battle scene -->
<!-- Cross-ref: outline.md § Interlude Finding Torren, npcs.md § Yara/Caden, locations.md § Roothollow -->

### 25a. The Dying Forest

*(The Thornmere Wilds. The ancient trees are petrifying — bark
turning to stone, leaves frozen mid-fall. The bioluminescent moss
is grey. Spirit-totems hang from branches, their carved faces
weeping grey sap. The path to Roothollow is still there, but the
forest around it is wrong — too still, too silent, like a held
breath.)*

*(Caden — the Duskfen spirit-speaker — stands at a crossing. He
looks exhausted.)*

**CADEN** : The ground is screaming. Can you not hear it? It has
been screaming for days.

**SABLE** : We're looking for Torren.

**CADEN** : At the nexus. He's been there since the rupture.
Performing a binding ritual. Pouring himself into the ward stones
to slow the corruption.

(If `edren_found` set.)
**EDREN** : How long can he sustain that?

**CADEN** : He can't. He's dying. But the alternative is losing
the nexus, and if that goes, the entire Wilds go with it.

### 25b. Roothollow

*(Roothollow. The great tree's roots are cracked. The interior
is dim — the moss barely glows. Families are evacuating, carrying
what they can through the root passages. Children cling to parents.
An elder directs the exodus with quiet efficiency.)*

*(Yara — Torren's apprentice, sixteen, circles under her eyes
that shouldn't be on a face that young — meets them at the
inner chamber.)*

**YARA** : You came. I told Torren someone would come. He said
the totems would have shaken if anyone was going to help.

**SABLE** : The totems are wrong.

**YARA** : I know. I can't sleep. The ley lines used to sing
in harmonics. Three voices, overlapping. Now there's only one
voice, and it's not singing. It's just... exhaling. Over and
over.

*(She leads them deeper, toward the heartwood shrine.)*

**YARA** : He's been at the nexus for eleven days. He stopped
eating on day three. I bring water. He doesn't always drink it.

### 25c. The Ley Nexus

*(The heartwood shrine. At its center, a ley nexus pulses with
sick grey light. Torren kneels before it, hands pressed to the
ward stones, his whole body trembling. Grey creeps up his arms
like frost. His eyes are closed. His lips move but no words
come out.)*

*(Around the nexus, the Ley Leech — a mass of grey corruption
anchored to the ley line itself — pulses and feeds. The trees
nearest the nexus are glass-still. Not dead. Emptied.)*

**SABLE** : Torren. Torren!

*(He doesn't respond. His hands stay on the stones.)*

(If `edren_found` set.)
**EDREN** : He's holding it back by himself. If he lets go—

(If `lira_found` set.)
**LIRA** : The Leech is anchored to the ley line. We have to
sever its connection before Torren can let go.

**SABLE** : Then we sever it.

<!-- Cross-ref: dungeons-world.md § Roothollow Ley Nexus (Ley Leech) -->

*(Boss fight: Ley Leech. The fight occurs around Torren, who
cannot move. Protecting him is implicit.)*

### 25d. Reunion

*(The Ley Leech dissolves. The grey light at the nexus flickers —
then steadies. Warm gold pulses through the ward stones. Clean
energy. The first clean ley energy since the rupture.)*

*(Torren collapses. Sable catches him.)*

**TORREN** : ...Sable?

**SABLE** : Yeah. Me. The useless one.

**TORREN** : You are many things. Useless is not one of them.

*(He lets her help him stand. The colour is already returning to
his arms — the grey frost receding.)*

**TORREN** : The nexus held. I felt it shift. Whatever you did—

**SABLE** : We killed the thing eating it. Pretty straightforward.

*(Torren [nod]. He looks at the ward stones — gold light, warm,
steady.)*

**TORREN** : This is the first sign. The Pallor can be pushed back.

(If `stag_bonded` set.)
*(A shimmer of light at the edge of the clearing. Grey-tinged but
recognizable — the Ley Stag reforms from motes of energy, antlers
faintly luminous, flanks marked with grey scars. It steps toward
Torren.)*

*(Torren reaches out. His hand touches the Stag's muzzle. It is
warm.)*

**TORREN** : You came back.

*(The Stag lowers its head. It didn't die. It waited.)*

*(Flag `stag_returned` (56) set.)*

*(Yara runs out from the root passages. She sees the golden nexus.
Her shoulders drop — tension held for eleven days releasing all
at once.)*

**YARA** : I can hear it again. The harmonics. Faint, but there.

*(She looks at Sable.)*

**YARA** : Thank you.

*(Flag `torren_found` (24) set. Torren rejoins the party.)*

## Scene 26: Finding Maren

<!-- Scene: finding_maren | Tier: 2+3 | Trigger: player travels to Deep Wilds -->
<!-- Location: Deep Wilds → Archive of Ages | Party: Sable + previously found members -->
<!-- Variants: reunion_order position -->
<!-- Cross-ref: outline.md § Interlude Finding Maren, npcs.md § Brenn/Riven/The Archivist, locations.md § Archive of Ages -->

### 26a. Brenn's Message

*(Deep forest. The kind of deep where the canopy is so thick the
floor is twilight at noon. Sable follows a trail marked with
old spirit-totems to a hermit's dwelling — a lean-to built
against a fallen tree, stacked with books that shouldn't survive
rain but do.)*

*(Brenn — former court mage, contemporary of Maren, opted for
obscurity over exile — looks up from his tea.)*

**BRENN** : Maren was the brilliant one. I was the other one. You
know — every generation has a Maren and a Brenn. Nobody remembers
the Brenn.

**SABLE** : She came through here?

**BRENN** : Two weeks ago. Left a message. Sealed, warded, very
dramatic. Typical Maren.

*(He produces a folded parchment that glows faintly at the edges.)*

**BRENN** : I didn't read it, if that's what you're wondering.
She'd know if I had.

*(Sable takes the message. It contains directions deeper into
the Wilds — toward a ruin older than any civilization on the map.)*

**BRENN** : There's a tracker who can guide you the rest of the
way. Riven. Runs alone in the deep places. Tell him Brenn sent
you. He'll pretend he doesn't know me, but he'll help.

### 26b. Riven

*(Deeper still. The trees here are ancient — trunks so wide they
could house families. The air smells of moss and age. Riven
stands on a root the size of a bridge, watching the party
approach.)*

**RIVEN** : I know who you're looking for. I know where she went.
The question is whether I lead you there or let you get lost and
die.

**SABLE** : Brenn sent us.

**RIVEN** : Brenn is a fool who lives in a lean-to and talks to
mushrooms. But he's a correct fool. This way.

*(He drops from the root and walks. He does not look back to see
if they follow.)*

### 26c. The Archive of Ages

*(The deep forest opens onto a structure that has no right to
exist — a building made of dark stone so precisely fitted that no
moss grows between the blocks. It is older than the trees. The
entrance is an archway without a door. Light comes from inside —
cold, constant, sourceless.)*

*(The party descends through corridors lined with records — not
books, not scrolls, but flat stone tablets arranged in rows that
stretch beyond sight. Each tablet is covered in text no living
language can parse.)*

*(At the deepest chamber, Maren sits cross-legged on the floor
surrounded by her own notes, ancient texts, and a figure that is
not quite human standing motionless in the corner.)*

*(The Archivist. Preserved by ancient magic. Its voice sounds
like multiple people talking at once, slightly out of sync.)*

**ARCHIVIST** : You are the sixth group to reach this chamber in
eleven thousand years. The previous five asked the same question.
The answer has not changed.

**SABLE** : Friendly place.

**MAREN** : It's not friendly. It's accurate. There's a
difference.

*(She stands. She looks exhausted but certain — the look of
someone who found what they were searching for and wishes they
hadn't.)*

**MAREN** : I found it. The pattern. The Pallor has been doing
this for ages — literally. Every cycle, a door opens. Every
cycle, someone is marked as its host. And every cycle, someone
has to close the door from the inside.

(If `edren_found` set.)
*(Edren [step_back]. He understands immediately.)*

(If `lira_found` set.)
*(Lira goes very still.)*

**SABLE** : From the inside.

**MAREN** : The hinge only turns one way.

*(She gestures at the Archivist.)*

**MAREN** : Ask it. Go ahead.

*(The Archivist speaks without being asked, as if it has been
waiting for this moment for millennia.)*

**ARCHIVIST** : The door closes from the inside. The one who
closes it does not return. This is not a curse. It is a
mechanical fact.

*(Silence.)*

**MAREN** : Every previous cycle, the knowledge was lost. The
next age started from nothing. That's why it keeps happening —
nobody remembers how it ended.

*(She picks up her notes. Shoves them into her bag with the
urgency of someone who understands she's carrying the most
important document in eleven thousand years.)*

**MAREN** : Not this time. I'm going to make sure the world
remembers.

*(Flag `maren_found` (26) set. Maren rejoins the party.)*

## Scene 27: The Full Briefing

<!-- Location: Safe camp (determined by last reunion location) | Party: Edren, Lira, Torren, Sable, Maren -->
<!-- Variants: reunion_order affects who references what -->
<!-- Cross-ref: outline.md § Interlude Maren's Briefing, events.md flag 27 -->

*(Night. A campfire in a safe clearing. The full party for the
first time since the siege. Five people, a fire, and the weight
of what they know.)*

*(Maren stands. She speaks plainly. No preamble.)*

**MAREN** : The Pendulum is a door. Cael opened it from this
side when he took the artifact. The Pallor — the Grey, the
Weight, whatever name you give it — is on the other side, and
it's been pushing through since the siege.

**MAREN** : The door closes from the inside. Someone who carries
the Pallor's mark has to choose to shut it. And they don't come
back.

*(Silence. The fire crackles.)*

**EDREN** : Cael is the one who's marked.

**MAREN** : Cael is the one who's marked.

*(No one says anything for a long time.)*

**LIRA** : Where is he?

**MAREN** : The Convergence. He's building something there — a
machine. I don't know what it does, but the Pallor energy is
concentrating on that point. If we're going to reach him, it
has to be now.

**TORREN** : The spirits won't go near the Convergence. They say
it sounds like the end of a sentence that never started.

**SABLE** : So we walk into the grey, fight through whatever's
waiting, find Cael, and... what? Ask him to close the door?

**MAREN** : We give him the choice. The Pallor took his choice
away. We give it back.

*(She meets each of their eyes in turn.)*

**MAREN** : That's all we can do.

**EDREN** : It's enough.

*(He stands. Draws his sword. Plants it in the dirt before the
fire.)*

**EDREN** : We go to the Convergence. We bring Cael back to
himself. Whatever comes after that — we face it together.

*(Torren [nod]. Lira [nod]. Sable closes her eyes, breathes,
opens them.)*

**SABLE** : Together. Yeah.

*(Flag `party_reassembled` (27) set. Convergence marked on world
map. Act III begins.)*

---

*The party is whole again. The truth is known. The Convergence
waits. And somewhere at its center, Cael is building something
that will either save the world or end it.*
