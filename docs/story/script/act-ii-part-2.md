<!-- Variants: none -->

*(Micro-cutscene. Brief. Disorienting. Plays mid-Act II when
the player enters certain areas of the Keep.)*

*(Cael's quarters. Night. He sits bolt upright in bed. Sweat.
Rapid breathing. The room is dark except for the faintest grey
light leaking beneath the vault door at the end of the corridor.)*

## Scene 17: Cael's Last Night

<!-- Scene: cael_last_night | Tier: 3 | Trigger: night before the siege -->
<!-- Location: Valdris Crown (4 locations) | Party: Cael (player-controlled, solo) -->
<!-- Variants: flags 44-47 set by which locations are visited (3 of 4) -->
<!-- Cross-ref: outline.md § Act II Cael's Last Night, events.md flags 44-47 -->

*(Night. The Keep is quiet. The player controls Cael — the only
time outside the Interlude where Edren is not the protagonist.
Four locations glow faintly on the map. Time allows three visits.
The player does not know this is a farewell.)*

### 17a. Lira's Workshop

<!-- If visited: flag cael_last_night_lira (44) set -->

*(The Forgewright workshop in the lower Keep. Oil and heat. Lira
works at her bench — adjusting a conduit, focus absolute. She
doesn't hear Cael enter.)*

**CAEL** : You're still up.

**LIRA** : This alignment won't set itself. Ley resonance
frequencies are off by point-three. If I don't recalibrate
before the energy cycles—

*(She stops. Looks at him.)*

**LIRA** : You look terrible.

**CAEL** : I'm fine.

**LIRA** : You've said that three times this week. What is it?

*(Cael sits on a workbench. He watches her — the way she holds
tools, the way she frowns at calculations, the way the forge-light
catches her face. He is memorizing.)*

**CAEL** : Do you ever think about what happens after?

**LIRA** : After what?

**CAEL** : After all of this. The Pendulum, the ley lines, the
politics. After it's done. What does the world look like?

**LIRA** : Better. The ley lines stabilize. The Forgewright craft
evolves instead of extracting. People build things that work with
the world instead of against it.

**CAEL** : And us?

*(Lira sets down her tools. Turns to face him fully.)*

**LIRA** : We figure it out. Same as everyone else.

*(Cael [turn_away]. Slight. She almost doesn't notice.)*

**CAEL** : Yeah. We figure it out.

*(He leaves. The workshop is quiet. Lira picks up her tools, but
her hands are slower now. Something unsaid hangs in the air.)*

### 17b. Training Grounds — Edren

<!-- If visited: flag cael_last_night_edren (45) set -->

*(The training yard. Empty at night. Torches gutter in brackets
along the walls. The sound of boots on packed earth. Edren is
there — running drills alone, sword work that's muscle memory.)*

**EDREN** : Cael. Can't sleep?

**CAEL** : Something like that.

**EDREN** : Grab a practice blade. It helps.

*(They spar. The rhythm is old, comfortable — attacks and parries
that come from years of shared training. No words for a while.
Just the clack of wood on wood.)*

**EDREN** : You've been spending too much time in that vault.

**CAEL** : Someone has to study it.

**EDREN** : Aldis can study it. You should rest.

**CAEL** : Aldis doesn't hear it the way I do.

*(Edren stops mid-swing. Studies Cael's face.)*

**EDREN** : Hear what?

*(Cael [head_down]. Beat. Then he smiles — a real smile, but sad
in a way Edren doesn't catch.)*

**CAEL** : Nothing. Just... the resonance patterns. They're
louder at night.

*(They resume. The sparring settles into a steady rhythm.)*

**EDREN** : Same time tomorrow?

*(Long pause.)*

**CAEL** : ...Same time tomorrow.

*(The pause breaks the moment. Edren should notice. He doesn't.
Or doesn't want to.)*

### 17c. Maren's Study

<!-- If visited: flag cael_last_night_maren (46) set -->

*(Candlelit study. Books, ley diagrams, artifacts from a dozen
civilizations. Maren writes at her desk. She looks up when Cael
enters — and something shifts in her expression. Wariness.)*

**MAREN** : Cael. It's late for a social call.

**CAEL** : I had questions. About your research on the Pallor's
previous cycles.

**MAREN** : What kind of questions?

**CAEL** : How long can someone hear the Pallor before it becomes
impossible to refuse?

*(Maren [bubble_ellipsis]. She sets her pen down.)*

**MAREN** : That depends on the individual. Weeks. Months.
Some resist for years. Why?

**CAEL** : Academic interest. And — can the Pallor reshape reality,
or only perception?

**MAREN** : Perception. It doesn't change what's real. It changes
what you're able to care about. The world stays the same. You
just stop seeing a reason to fight for it.

**CAEL** : And what if the Pendulum doesn't empower the Pallor,
but actually contains it? What if someone could use the connection
to — redirect it?

*(Maren stands.)*

**MAREN** : Those are not academic questions, Cael.

*(She studies his face. He meets her eyes — calm, certain, a
scholar pursuing a hypothesis.)*

**CAEL** : Maren. I'm trying to understand what we're dealing
with. That's all.

**MAREN** : The Pallor doesn't make deals. It doesn't negotiate.
It offers what you want most, and the price is always the same —
you stop fighting. Whatever you're thinking, stop thinking it.

*(Cael [nod]. Polite. Controlled. He leaves.)*

*(Maren watches the door close. Her expression shifts from wary
to afraid. She picks up her pen, but doesn't write.)*

### 17d. The Pendulum Vault

<!-- If visited: flag cael_last_night_vault (47) set -->

*(The vault. Underground. Glass containment case. Containment
wards glow faintly blue. The Pendulum sits inside — small, brass,
its needle still.)*

*(Cael stands before the case. No dialogue. The silence is total.)*

*(He places his hand on the glass. His reflection stares back.)*

*(For a single frame — a flicker so brief a player might not
catch it — his reflection is wreathed in grey light. The eyes
are not his. The posture is not his. Something else looks back.)*

*(Cael withdraws his hand. His own eyes — clear, brown, human —
blink once in the glass.)*

*(He turns and walks away. Behind him, the Pendulum's needle
shifts. Two degrees. Holds.)*

### 17e. The Corridor — Between Visits

*(Between locations, Cael walks the Keep's corridors alone. The
ley-lamps flicker. His shadow falls wrong — too long, too still,
as if something else is casting it alongside him.)*

*(After visiting the first location:)*

**CAEL** : *(internal)* One more. I can see one more before—

*(He presses his palm against his temple. Breathes.)*

*(After visiting the second location:)*

**CAEL** : *(internal)* The whisper is louder tonight. Not words.
A feeling. Like the floor is tilting and everyone else is standing
straight but you know it's tilting.

*(He passes a window. Moonlight. His reflection walks a half-step
behind him.)*

*(After visiting the third and final location:)*

*(Cael stands in the corridor outside the vault. He has one visit
left and no time. He places his hand on the wall. The stone is
cold.)*

**CAEL** : *(internal)* Enough. It's time.

*(He walks toward the vault. The ley-lamps in the corridor go
dark behind him, one by one, as if the light itself is giving
up.)*

## Scene 18: The Betrayal

<!-- Scene: cael_betrayal | Tier: 1 | Trigger: cael_betrayal_complete (flag 19) -->
<!-- Location: Valdris Crown inner ward | Party: all six (Cael departing) -->
<!-- Variants: flags 44-47 affect camera framing (see variants section) -->
<!-- Cross-ref: outline.md § Act II The Betrayal, events.md flag 19 -->

*(Dawn. Alarm bells. But not from the walls — from inside the
Keep. [SFX: alarm_bells])*

*(The party converges on the inner ward. The Pendulum vault door
is open. The containment wards are shattered — not broken from
outside, but unraveled from within, methodically, by someone who
understood their architecture.)*

*(Cael stands in the courtyard. The Pendulum is in his hand. He
is calm. His armour is buckled for travel. His eyes are clear.
That is the worst part.)*

**EDREN** : Cael. Put it down.

**CAEL** : I can't do that.

**LIRA** : What are you doing?

*(Cael looks at Lira. His expression does not change — it is the
look of someone who has already grieved what he is about to
destroy.)*

**CAEL** : I'm ending this. The Pendulum isn't a weapon and it
isn't a cage. It's a key. And I know how to use it.

**MAREN** : The Pallor is using you. Whatever it promised—

**CAEL** : It didn't promise anything. It showed me.

**CAEL** : Every cycle for eleven thousand years, someone finds
this thing, someone opens the door, and someone pays the price
to close it.

**CAEL** : Every time, the world forgets. Every time, it happens
again.

*(He steps back.)*

**CAEL** : I'm going to break the cycle. Not close the door —
destroy it.

**CAEL** : The machine at the Convergence can channel enough
Pallor energy to shatter the connection permanently. No more
door. No more cycles. No more cost.

**EDREN** : You don't know that. You're listening to something
that wants you to believe—

**CAEL** : I'm listening to the only thing that's telling me the
truth. The rest of you are too afraid to hear it.

*(Cael [turn_away]. His signature. He cannot face them for what
comes next.)*

**CAEL** : I'm doing this for you. For everyone.

(If `cael_last_night_lira` set.)
*(Camera lingers on Lira's face. She knew. She didn't know she
knew, but she knew.)*

(If `cael_last_night_edren` set.)
*(Camera lingers on Edren. The pause. "Same time tomorrow." He
heard it and chose not to hear it.)*

(If `cael_last_night_maren` set.)
*(Camera shifts to Maren. Her fear from last night crystallizes.
The questions weren't research. They were a plan.)*

(If `cael_last_night_vault` set.)
*(Flash: Cael's reflection in the vault glass. Grey eyes.
Something else looking back. The contact was already made.)*

*(Lira steps forward.)*

(If `cael_last_night_lira` set.)
**LIRA** : Last night. In the workshop. You were saying goodbye.

**CAEL** : I was trying not to.

**LIRA** : Cael. Please. Stay.

*(He does not turn around.)*

**CAEL** : I love you. I loved you in the workshop and I loved
you on the castle walls and I love you now. That's why I can't.

(If `cael_last_night_lira` NOT set.)
**LIRA** : Cael. Please. Stay.

*(He does not turn around. He didn't visit her last night. He
couldn't. That tells her everything.)*

**CAEL** : I love you. That's why I can't.

*(Grey light envelops him — the Pendulum's power answering his
call. He vanishes. The courtyard is empty. The alarm bells
continue.)*

*(Sable [head_down]. Torren [step_back]. Maren [bubble_ellipsis].
Edren stands perfectly still, staring at the space where Cael
was.)*

*(Flag `cael_betrayal_complete` (19) set. Cael removed from
party.)*

## Scene 19: The Siege of Valdris

<!-- Scene: siege_of_valdris | Tier: 1 → 3 | Trigger: carradan_assault_begins (flag 15) -->
<!-- Location: Valdris Crown — walls, lower ward, inner ward, throne hall -->
<!-- Party: Edren, Lira, Torren, Sable, Maren -->
<!-- Variants: council_result (flag 43) affects allied support at walls -->
<!-- Cross-ref: outline.md § Act II Siege, bestiary/bosses.md § Ashen Ram / Vaelith, events.md flags 15-18 -->

### 19a. The Assault

*(Hours later. The party has barely processed Cael's departure
when the horizon fills with dust. The Carradan Compact's southern
army — siege engines, infantry columns, and at their center, the
Ashen Ram: a massive Forgewright siege engine belching smoke and
grinding forward on iron treads.)*

*(Dame Cordwyn meets them on the wall. [SFX: drums_war])*

**CORDWYN** : They've been mobilizing for weeks. The betrayal
wasn't a signal — it was a distraction. While we watched Cael
run, they marched.

**EDREN** : How long do we have?

**CORDWYN** : The Ram hits the eastern wall within the hour.
We need everyone on the battlements.

(If `council_result` >= 2.)
*(Thornmere archers arrive from the forest road — silent, fast,
bows already strung. Caden leads them.)*

**CADEN** : We came. The spirits said to hurry.

(If `council_result` == 0.)
*(No allied support. The walls are manned only by Valdris
soldiers. Cordwyn's face says everything.)*

**CORDWYN** : We hold with what we have.

### 19b. The Ashen Ram

<!-- Cross-ref: bestiary/bosses.md § The Ashen Ram -->

*(The eastern wall. The Ashen Ram grinds forward — a Forgewright
war machine the size of a building, smoke and fire pouring from
its vents. Behind it, Compact infantry advance in formation.)*

*(Boss fight: The Ashen Ram. Lv 22, 25,000 HP. 3 phases.)*

*(The Ram breaches the eastern wall. Stone explodes inward.
The Lower Ward is exposed. [SFX: wall_breach])*

### 19c. King Aldren's Death

*(The inner ward. The fighting has pushed inward. King Aldren
stands in the throne hall corridor with a handful of guards.
He holds a sword — not well, but with the grip of a man who
learned once and remembers enough.)*

*(Compact soldiers breach the corridor. Aldren stands his ground.)*

**KING ALDREN** : I was a poor king. I know that. But I will not
be a poor man.

*(He fights. He falls. It is not dramatic — it is sudden, messy,
a sword through armor where armor is thin. He falls against the
wall and slides down. The guards are overwhelmed.)*

*(The party arrives moments too late.)*

**EDREN** : Your Majesty—

*(Aldren's eyes find whoever reaches him first.)*

(If Edren reaches him first.)
**KING ALDREN** : Edren. The crown... was always too heavy. You
understand that now. Protect... what's left.

(If Lira reaches him first.)
**KING ALDREN** : The Forgewright... Maren was right. The ley
lines... should have listened...

(If Maren reaches him first.)
**KING ALDREN** : Maren. I should have listened. When you told
me... about the door...

**MAREN** : I know, Your Majesty.

**KING ALDREN** : Don't let it... open further...

(Default — Edren and party arrive together.)
**KING ALDREN** : Protect... what's left.

*(He dies. No grand speech. No wisdom. Just a man who ran out of
time. The crown sits on the floor where it fell, gold dulled with
dust.)*

*(Flag `king_aldren_dead` (17) set.)*

### 19d. Vaelith on the Wall

<!-- Cross-ref: bestiary/bosses.md § Vaelith (Siege) -->

*(The broken eastern wall. Fire. Smoke. The party pushes through
to the breach point and finds Vaelith standing atop the rubble.
Not fighting. Not commanding. Watching.)*

**VAELITH** : You are doing wonderfully. Truly. I have not been
this entertained in centuries.

*(The party attacks. Vaelith moves like water — casual, effortless.
Every strike is deflected. Every ability absorbed. This is not a
fight. It's a demonstration.)*

*(Scripted loss. The party cannot win. Vaelith does not kill them
— they simply overwhelm them until consciousness fades.)*

**VAELITH** : Rest now. You'll need your strength. The real work
hasn't started yet.

*(Blackout. [SFX: pallor_surge])*

*(The party wakes in the aftermath. Valdris is burning. The king
is dead. Cael is gone. The Pendulum is gone.)*

*(Flag `vaelith_siege_encounter` (18) set.)*

*(Act II ends.)*

plan — burns. The king is dead. The friend is gone. The artifact
is in the hands of something that wears a familiar face. And the
Pendulum, wherever it is now, is closer to the door than ever.*
